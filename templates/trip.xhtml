Break out the Bubbly

There's something special about California sparkling wine. Here are our top stops for bubbles in Napa & Sonoma

  • Transportation
  • Directions
  • Share
Save Trip
  • Distance 13 Miles
  • Total Time Hrs

Overview

The roster of Northern California bubbly houses with open tasting rooms is small enough that you could easily visit them all. There are many others -- Ram’s Gate, Artesa and Inman Family, for example -- who contract for small amounts of sparkling wine to be made at other facilities. They’re great wines, but visiting those wineries doesn’t offer the full experience of a working sparkling-wine production facility. One of the joys of visiting these dedicated Champagne-method sparkling producers is getting to glimpse the fascinating production process. This is an instance where you’d be wise to opt for a tour.

Schramsberg Vineyards

1400 Schramsberg Rd, Calistoga, CA
Add to Trip

The historic property in Calistoga — which first became a winery in the 1860s, under the stewardship of German barber Jacob Schram — has been owned since 1965 by the Davies family, who established themselves as a premier producer of Champagne-method sparkling wine. They remain among very few major California bubbly producers that is not owned by a French Champagne company. Read the full review.

Gloria Ferrer Caves & Vineyards

23555 Arnold Drive, Sonoma, CA 95476, United States
Add to Trip

Luxuries include table service for the flight tastings, ranging from to , either inside or on the Vista Terrace overlooking the vineyards. Either way, it's interesting to learn about the intricate méthode champenoise process using estate Chardonnay and Pinot Noir grapes. This, after all, is the first sparkling wine house in Carneros, built in 1986, with an overall history dating back 600 years to the Ferrer family’s Spanish roots. No wonder it’s one of Sonoma Valley’s grandest experiences. Read the full review.

Circle Sonoma's West Side

Max Theirot of Senses Wines takes us on a tour of the West County Country-side

Add All Save Trip

  • Share

Overview

"A loop through some of my favorite west county back roads is a great way to see the countryside and sip some of my favorite wines.
Leave the town of Sebastopol and head west on highway 12 where you will come to Gold Ridge Road. Be sure to take a left to get to my first stop, Littorai Estate (Note: Reservations are required). You will not be disappointed when you walk onto the estate: the wines are terrific and, if you're interested, they offer an amazing, biodynamic farm tour experience.

After Littorai, continue heading west on highway 12 and you will come to the town of Freestone. Joseph Phelps has a fun tasting room here where I like to stop. Another perk is the awesome bakery next door. Be sure to grab a loaf before you leave!

Next, continue on Bohemian Hwy into my hometown of Occidental. Its a great spot to stop for lunch at one of the many restaurants. After lunch, don't miss one of my favorites, Boheme Wines, which happens to have a "cellar door" tasting room right in the heart of the town. On the weekends there's a good chance you can find the winemaker himself, Kurt Beitler, pouring the wines (which are fantastic). Kurt and his family have a long history of making great wines in the Sonoma/Napa area.

Lastly, head out of town and back towards Santa Rosa. As you drive east on Graton Road, you will come to the town of Graton. There are a lot of great places to finish the day near here, but two of my favorites (which happen to be very close to each other) are Dutton Goldfield and Merry Edwards." –Max Theirot, January 2017
